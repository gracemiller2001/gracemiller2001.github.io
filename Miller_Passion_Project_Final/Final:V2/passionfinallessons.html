Country: Mexico

Mexico City:

The beauty of Mexico City is hard to beat. While one might prefer to view the latest print travel guide, YouTube can also serve as a great reference for itineraries, etc. Depending on your interests, you can become familiar with the city via multi-hour virtual walking tours, or watch influencers or travel companies like Expedia guide your way.

Additional Materials by Theme:

My Go-To's for every content area.

Music:

This podcast is a definite go-to for learning about the music of Mexico, and the Southern United States, especially, Texas. Learn about the queen, Selena , her most famous musical genres of cumbia and tejano, and the impact of her life in all aspects of society – the media, art, etc.

Dance:

Kelly Marcelino teaches a variety of Latin dances and dance steps in reel-format on her Instagram ! A personal favorite of mine, Kelly Marcelino's page can help anyone learn how to cumbia, salsa, etc.!

Food:

In addition to the fantastic documentary, "Taco Chronicles" on Netflix, the documentary, "La Divina Gula", or "Heavenly Bites", is a great way to learn about the gastronomy of Mexico, as the chefs' creativity takes classics like tortas to the next level.

Travel:

It's safe to say that Lonely Planet has outdone themselves online as a leader in travel. Not only does the site offer stunning photography, but it also lists tours, called "Lonely Planet Experiences"!

Even during the school year, I find that I can still immerse myself to the best of my ability in the language and culture, even if that means having a Topo Chico and a Casa Nueva quesadilla on a Friday!
